Third Workshop@UH on Security and Privacy

We are organizing a one-day workshop at University of Houston, Department of Computer Science. The Workshop will include presentations on Security modules and supporting materials, as well as some hands-on activities. The third workshop will be held on 11th August, Saturday.

Third workshop at UH on 11th August, 2018:

Travel Awards and/or stipends will be available to Faculty, PostDocs and advanced PhD students (1 year from graduation), who are interested in adopting/adapting the coursewares we have developed. This workshop is not limited to CS people. We invite applications from Social Science, Mathematics, Economics and Engineering Departments.
The links to the Registration and Travel Support forms.
The workshop will discuss security and privacy issues of broader significance as well as technical challenges.

WORKSHOP FOCUS:

  • Introduce the Enriching Security Curricula and enhancing awareness of Security project (http://capex.cs.uh.edu/)
  • Introduce instructors to modules and hands on practice on topics on Security Analytics and Privacy in Social Networks
  • Build a community of practitioners that contributes to enhancing security awareness across and beyond computer science

BENEFITS:

At the end of the workshop, participants will be able to:

  • Readily integrate the modules offered into relevant activities for CS/IT courses
  • Use the modules content to improve and enhance students learning in these topics
  • Participate in building a community that uses and develops learning material in a modular form to enhance student learning.

SUPPORT:

The workshop is supported by an NSF grant. Funding is available to support travel and lodging for a limited number of participants.
